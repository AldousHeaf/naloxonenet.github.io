The Challenge

Over the past five years, an annual average of 3,238 adolescents have died due to unexpected overdoses. In two-thirds of these deaths, bystanders were present.

The CDC has stated that reducing overdose fatalities requires increasing bystander access to naloxone and administration training. Anyone, in any place, could find themselves in the position of an overdose bystander, and NaloxoneNet empowers them to act.

Currently, naloxone is administered by emergency responders in 96.6% of cases. Yet over half of victims have no pulse by the time these medics arrive. NaloxoneNet aims to significantly reduce this number through crowdsourcing, while also minimizing the stress and responsibility placed on initial responders.

How NaloxoneNet Works

Interactive Map

Color-coded pins show all local naloxone locations:

  • Green: Free naloxone available
  • Yellow: Available for purchase

Includes pharmacies, publicly funded facilities, stores, and bars with complete address, hours, and contact information.

AI Administration Assistant

A reasoning model algorithm that mimics human thinking, providing real-time, tailored responses to questions about naloxone administration.

The chatbot uses a simplified LLM from LangChain to break down complex tasks, helping users understand and execute proper administration techniques—even under extreme stress.

Emergency Services Integration

Quick access to call emergency response services or find directions to the nearest hospital. The app will not automatically call 911 unless explicitly authorized by the user.

Community-Driven Growth

Users can add locations in their communities, making NaloxoneNet more valuable as more people contribute. This crowdsourcing model ensures comprehensive coverage without ongoing maintenance costs.

The Opportunity

If bystanders had been properly equipped with naloxone and training, they could have provided an initial overdose response, potentially preventing nearly 67% of deaths during the 2019-2021 period.

NaloxoneNet bridges this critical gap by providing immediate access to both the medication and the knowledge needed to administer it, potentially saving thousands of lives annually.

Our Story

Mission

To empower bystanders with immediate access to naloxone locations and real-time administration guidance, dramatically reducing fatal overdoses through technology and community collaboration.

Vision

A world where no one dies from an overdose when bystanders are present. We envision communities where naloxone access is universal, administration knowledge is widespread, and lives are saved every day.

Values

We believe in universal access to life-saving resources, community-driven solutions, and empowering individuals with knowledge and tools. Every feature we build prioritizes saving lives and reducing barriers to care.

Market Opportunity

The greatest advantage of this innovation is its universal relevance. While NaloxoneNet is critical for individuals concerned about their own overdose safety, it also serves a broader audience by empowering users to intervene when they encounter someone else overdosing.

Overdoses frequently occur in everyday settings—concerts, parties, dorm rooms, and private homes—often in the presence of bystanders. Our app functions as a safeguard, offering value with no downside to downloading it, even for users who do not anticipate regular use.

Initial Launch Markets

NaloxoneNet will initially launch in major college towns such as Ann Arbor, MI, Austin, TX, and Orlando, FL, with easy scalability to additional cities and states.

Competitive Advantage

Unlike state-run websites that are underdeveloped or limited by borders, NaloxoneNet provides both web and app access with more comprehensive data. Our AI support feature centralizes necessary information, eliminating inefficient text instructions and calming users during crisis situations. Unlike PulsePoint (AED locator), we require no community sponsorship or complex implementation process.
